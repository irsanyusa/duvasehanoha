Top 10 advantages and disadvantages of friction: what are they?

July 01, 2024
blog

You might not realise it, but friction is vital to your everyday life. You come across it everywhere you go, literally. Even when you are sitting down, standing up or walking, the force of friction affects you. There are both advantages and disadvantages of friction. Find out why friction is good and bad at the same time.

Friction is the force that arises from the contact of two bodies and prevents their relative movement. The cause of friction is the roughness of rubbing surfaces and the interaction of the molecules of these surfaces.

What is friction?

Friction is a force that opposes the relative motion or tendency of such motion between two surfaces in contact.

The advantages and disadvantages of friction

The frictional force depends on the material of the rubbing surfaces and on how intensely these surfaces are pressed against each other. Friction comes in a few different forms:

  • Static friction describes the process between the resting object and a surface.
  • Rolling friction appears between a rolling object and a surface.
  • Fluid friction occurs when objects move through liquid or gas.
  • Sliding friction comes into action when an object slides over a surface.

What are the main advantages of friction?

Friction offers several advantages in various aspects of your daily life. Here are the ten advantages of friction in physics and everyday life.

1. Movement

Friction between your shoes and the ground allows you to walk and run without slipping. This is essential for your everyday mobility.

2. Piling of objects

Putting objects on top of each other is an excellent way to store items without too much floor space. A friction force acts between the surfaces of the two items, preventing the objects from sliding and, therefore, allowing piling.

3. Machining and cutting

In machining and cutting processes, friction plays a role in removing material from workpieces. Cutting tools rely on friction to perform their functions effectively.

4. Traction

Friction between tires and the road surface provides the necessary traction for vehicles to move safely. This is crucial for preventing skidding and accidents.

5. Generating heat

The frictional force between two surfaces produces heat. This phenomenon is exploited in various equipment, including a spin welding process that requires heating. When you're cold, you frequently rub your hands together. The rubbing of palms creates friction, which generates heat, causing your hands to heat up.

6. Fastening

Screws, bolts, and nuts use solid friction to hold objects together. The threads create friction, preventing them from coming loose and ensuring secure connections.

7. Turning and steering

Friction between the tires and the road allows vehicles to turn and steer in a controlled manner. While turning, the friction between the brake pads and the rotors or drums can be adjusted to help control the vehicle's speed and direction.

8. Lighting up a fire

Friction was pivotal in humanity's discovery of fire in ancient times. Early humans harnessed the power of friction to ignite fires by vigorously rubbing dry wooden branches or stones together. The act of rubbing two objects generated heat, and this friction-induced heat initiated a chain of reactions that eventually resulted in the creation of fire.

9. Clutch operation

In vehicles with manual transmissions, the clutch relies on friction to engage and disengage the engine from the transmission, allowing for gear changes and smooth driving.

10. Writing and drawing

Friction between the pen or pencil and paper is necessary for writing and drawing. It helps leave a mark on the paper as you apply force.

What are the main disadvantages of friction?

Here are some of the main disadvantages of friction.

  • Friction converts kinetic energy (movement) into thermal energy (heat). This energy loss can lower mechanical system efficiency and necessitate additional input to overcome frictional forces.
  • It produces wear and tear on moving parts, resulting in component degradation and the need for maintenance or replacement.
  • Friction in machinery and mechanical systems can degrade system efficiency by increasing the amount of energy required to overcome it.
  • Friction produces heat, which can be harmful in some applications where excessive heat might cause overheating, material damage, or fire concerns.
  • Noise and vibration can be produced by friction between moving parts, which is undesirable in some contexts, such as precision machinery or quiet environments.
  • Excessive friction can reduce control and make it difficult to perform precise movements or maintain stability.
  • Friction often requires additional force or effort to overcome. For example, pushing a heavy object across a rough surface requires more effort due to friction.
  • Friction can slow down moving objects, reducing the momentum and making it challenging to maintain consistent performance.
  • In certain situations, such as when there is excessive friction between materials, it can generate sparks and potentially cause fires, which can be dangerous in industrial settings.
  • It can cause injuries due to slips and falls. This mostly occurs when the surface is slippery, and there may not be enough friction to prevent the foot from slipping. This lack of traction can result in a sudden loss of balance and lead to a slip and fall.

What are the advantages of solid friction?

The friction between two solid surfaces in contact is known as solid friction. It has a number of advantages and practical applications in a variety of sectors. They are as follows:

  • Solid friction allows us to grip and handle objects effectively. It would be challenging to hold, manipulate, or pick up objects without friction.
  • Solid friction is integral to various sports and recreational activities. For instance, it provides grip in rock climbing, baseball, and weightlifting.
  • Solid friction is used in processes such as sanding, polishing, and grinding in manufacturing and industrial settings to achieve specific surface finishes and material removal.

What is the law of solid friction?

The law of friction states that the force of friction acts in the direction opposite to the motion or potential motion of the surface. When the surface is at rest, the force of friction is equal in magnitude to the force applied to the surfaces.

What are the factors affecting friction?

The major factors affecting friction are the adhesion between body surfaces, roughness of the surface and deformation of bodies.
