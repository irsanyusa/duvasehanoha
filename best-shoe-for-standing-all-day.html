10 Best Shoes for Standing All Day 2024

blog

Whether you’re standing for the majority of the day at work, for long periods of time when running errands, or attending activities with family and friends, a solid pair of shoes is essential to keep your feet comfortable, protected, and sweat-free.

The best shoes for standing all day “often have ergonomic designs that offer excellent arch support and promote proper alignment, preventing foot and leg pain,” says Suzanne C. Fuchs, a board-certified Florida-based podiatrist at Luxe Podiatry. Shoe shopping can feel overwhelming with the vast amount of shoes in the market claiming to offer leading foot support and protection. To make your shoe shopping process easier, we’ve compiled a list of the best places to buy shoes made for standing all day. In addition to Fuchs, we spoke with Heidi M. Godoy, DPM of Godoy Foot & Ankle Care Center about what makes certain shoes better for standing all day. They also provided us with some shoe recommendations which we considered in addition to some of the pairs we’ve worn before.

How we selected the best shoes for standing all day

Support

When it comes to choosing the best shoes for standing all day, “look for shoes that have a firm midsole and arch support as this helps to maintain proper alignment and reduces strain on your feet, ankles, and legs,” says Fuchs.

Cushioning

Searching for shoes with adequate cushioning is essential, especially when they’re a pair you plan on wearing during periods of prolonged standing. “Look for shoes with cushioned insoles or midsoles made from materials like memory foam or gel,” suggests Fuchs. “These materials provide excellent shock absorption and enhance overall comfort.”

Breathability

Breathability is important when it comes to the shoes you’ll be wearing when standing all day as your feet are likely to become sweaty. A shoe’s breathability may be increased when it’s “made from breathable materials like mesh or perforated leather as these materials promote airflow, prevent moisture buildup, and reduce the risk of foot odor and fungal infections,” explains Fuchs.

Stability

Fuchs emphasizes the importance of stability when it comes to the shoes you’ll be wearing when standing all day to stay balanced and prevent excessive or unwanted foot movement. “Look for shoes with a wider base and a firm heel counter,” says Fuchs. “A wider base provides better stability, while a firm heel counter helps to keep your foot in place and prevents excessive pronation or supination.”

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Which type of shoes is best for standing all day?

According to Fuchs, sneakers or athletic shoes are the best type of shoes for standing all day. “They provide cushioning, support, and shock absorption, which help to reduce foot fatigue and discomfort.”

What are the best shoes for standing all day with flat feet?

If you have flat feet or are shopping for the best shoes for standing all day for someone who does, the best shoes to look for are the ones that offer good arch support. “Look for shoes with built-in arch support or consider using orthotic inserts to provide additional support,” says Fuchs.

What are the best shoes for standing over 12 hours?

If you’re somebody who regularly stands on your feet for more than 12 hours at a time or has future plans to do so, it is important to pick shoes that will keep your feet in the best possible condition. “When standing for over 12 hours, it is crucial to choose shoes that prioritize comfort and support,” explains Fuchs. “Sneakers with ample cushioning and shock absorption are recommended.” Additionally, shoes with a supportive midsole and a breathable upper can help prevent foot fatigue and discomfort.

Why do my feet hurt after standing all day?

If you’re shopping for the best shoes for standing all day, you more than likely have noticed that your feet hurt by the end of the day. There are many reasons why your feet might hurt after standing all day. “Prolonged standing can cause muscle fatigue and strain on the feet, leading to discomfort and pain,” explains Fuchs. “Furthermore, the lack of proper support and cushioning in shoes can exacerbate this issue. Additionally, standing for long periods can also lead to poor circulation, swelling, and inflammation in the feet.”
