The dating show has been airing on Channel 4, a British broadcast network, since 2016, but now “Naked Attraction” has been quietly added to Max — which starts at $9.99/month. Not a subscriber? You can still watch the risqué show with a free seven-day trial via Prime Video.

According to the censored trailer (shown above), “Naked Attraction” promises to “start where a good date often ends — naked.”

Here’s how it works: Hosted by British TV personality Anna Richardson, a single “chooser” picks a dating partner from a pool of six naked people who are hidden inside of colorful semi-transparent booths. As bodies are slowly revealed from the feet up until their faces are shown, the chooser eliminates each potential date based on their physical features until only two (or three) remain. The chooser then takes off their clothes so the potential dates can critique their body before a final choice is made. The pair (and even sometimes the trio) then go on a date, fully clothed, and come back to talk about their romantic time together.

If you dare, the first six seasons of “Naked Attraction” — which is very, very explicit — are available to stream on Max (the seventh season is currently airing in the U.K.) with up to 10 episodes per season. You can watch the dating show for free with a seven-day trial from Prime Video.
